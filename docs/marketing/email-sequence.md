# Waitlist Email Sequence — BJJ Open Mat

> Goal: turn a waitlist signup into an engaged beta tester and a launch-day installer + rater.
> Keep emails short, plain-text-feeling, and from a person ("Davis") — not a brand no-reply.
> Send from a real reply-to address. One CTA per email. Segment: **practitioners** vs **gym owners**.

**Sequence at a glance**
| # | Trigger | Timing | Subject | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| E1 | On signup | Instant | You're on the founding list 🥋 | Confirm + set expectations |
| E2 | E1 + 2 days | Day 2 | Why I built BJJ Open Mat | Story + trust |
| E3 | E1 + 5 days | Day 5 | Quick ask: where do you train? | Engagement + data |
| E4 | Beta build ready | On trigger | You're in — here's your beta invite | Convert to tester |
| E5 | Beta + 4 days | On trigger | How's it going? (2-min favor) | Feedback + bug reports |
| E6 | Launch − 3 days | On trigger | We go live Tuesday. Be first in. | Warm the launch |

| G1 | Owner signup | Instant | Your gym's spot on the map 🏅 | Owner onboarding |

## Sending notes
- Tool: any ESP (the waitlist tool's built-in email, or Mailchimp/ConvertKit/Loops). Plain, personal formatting beats heavy templates here.
- **Segment on signup** (practitioner vs. gym owner) so owners get G1 + owner-flavored launch emails.
- Add UTM tags to every link. Track open + reply rate; replies are your best signal in a small community.
- Keep cadence gentle — over-emailing a beta list churns it. E1–E3 pre-beta, E4–E5 during beta, E6–E7 at launch.
