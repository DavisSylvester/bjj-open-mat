## Follow-up maker comments (seed the thread — post over the day)

**On the "why now" question:**
> BJJ participation is booming and the whole culture runs on open mats — free, social, travel-friendly
> training — but there's no dedicated way to find them. People rely on scattered Facebook posts and
> word of mouth. This is the map + social layer for that.

**On "how is this different from a gym finder / Google Maps":**
> A map pin tells you a gym exists. It doesn't tell you whether anyone will actually be there. We show
> *who's going* to a specific session, with belt rank, so you can RSVP and walk into a new gym knowing
> what you're getting into. That social layer is the whole point.

**On gym owners:**
> For gyms it's free exposure — travelers and locals find your open mat, RSVP, and show up, and you
> see expected attendance before the doors open. First 100 gyms get a Founding Gym badge.
